This guide is for Mac OS X. Actually, it’s more of a personal reminder in case my computer crashes (or I get a new one) and I have to set everything up again.

For long work sessions, comfort is imperative.

This goes beyond comfy chairs (I actually hate expensive “office chairs” and prefer hard ones that don’t recline). Even when my eyes aren’t glued to a screen, I’m a bit particular about the ambient lighting in whatever environment I happen to be in.

And when it comes to screens, the blue light normally emitted by monitors and other screens negatively affects your sleep patterns. I also got LASIK less than a year ago, so small text on a bright glowing screen isn’t exactly my eyes’ forté these days.

And we all know how much more fun programming is when you have a fly setup.

The following is how I have set up my writing and computing environment to make working (or even just casual computer usage) comfortable and pleasant, no matter the time of day or length of the session.

You may be interested in implementing some of these even if you aren’t a programmer or even a blogger who writes in Markdown. I will put an avocado 🥑 next to general-purpose items. (For example, Atom is also a great general-purpose text editor and brilliant for writing in Markdown.)

Display 🥑

f.lux: If you haven’t heard of this, you need this. Keep those harmful blue light rays from disturbing your sleep, or even just from making you recoil in horror when you get up in the middle of the night to check something.

I mean, yeah, the latest MacOS now has native Night Shift mode, but from what I’ve read, it’s not as subtle or effective.

Browser 🥑

Install Stylus (for Chrome, Firefox, and Opera) and grab

  • Google Dark Mode
  • Wikipedia Dark Mode
  • GitHub Dark Mode
  • Reddit & YouTube already have a dark mode feature 👍🏻

I’m sure there are plenty of other themes, but this covers most of my needs. My own blog should have a dark mode, though 🙈

Markdown

Install language-gfm-enhanced (supports more languages and \(\LaTeX\))

pip3 install markdown-kernel
python3 -m markdown_kernel.install

Change Language Mappings in Hydrogen settings to: 

{ "python": "magicpython", "markdown": "github markdown"}

This setup highlights delimited \(\LaTeX\) and \(\KaTeX\) properly within Markdown files, but rendering is a little slow (i.e., you might get a blank preview the first time), and the \(\KaTeX\)-specific syntax is not supported by the renderer.
